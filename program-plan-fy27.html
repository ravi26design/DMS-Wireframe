1. Executive Summary

This Program Plan outlines the strategic priorities, resource allocation, and operational milestones for FES programs in the fiscal year 2026–27. It consolidates inputs from field teams, thematic leads, and the finance department to provide a unified framework for program delivery.

The plan targets five core thematic areas — Forest & Biodiversity, Water Governance, Agriculture & Livelihoods, Community Land Rights, and Institutional Strengthening — across 14 states and over 180 active program villages.

Key Outcome Target FY27: Strengthen ecological security of 2.4 lakh hectares of community land through improved governance, inclusive planning, and sustainable resource management.

2. Program Objectives

  • Expand community-based natural resource management (CBNRM) to 40 new village clusters
  • Complete NTFP value-chain documentation across 6 landscape corridors
  • Facilitate formation of 120 new Joint Forest Management Committees (JFMCs)
  • Deliver capacity-building workshops to 1,200+ grassroots leaders
  • Publish 3 policy briefs on land tenure and forest rights
  • Achieve ≥85% utilization of allocated program budget by Q4 FY27

4. Key Activities — Q1 to Q4

Q1 (Apr – Jun 2026)

  • Kick-off meetings in all 14 state offices
  • Annual Program Review (APR) document finalization
  • Field staff orientation on FY27 targets
  • Budget release to state teams

Q2 (Jul – Sep 2026)

  • Mid-year field monitoring visits (40% coverage)
  • JFMC formation drives — Odisha, MP, Chhattisgarh
  • NTFP assessment surveys in 6 corridors
  • Financial audit — Q1 expenditure

Q3 (Oct – Dec 2026)

  • National Program Review Workshop (2 days)
  • Policy brief drafts circulated for internal review
  • Community land documentation — Maharashtra pilot
  • Capacity building workshops (batch 2 of 3)

Q4 (Jan – Mar 2027)

  • Final field visits and outcome documentation
  • Annual report data aggregation
  • FY28 pre-planning consultations
  • Year-end financial closure and audit
